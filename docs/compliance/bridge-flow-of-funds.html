1. Intended use-case

Sanova Capital is a marketplace where KYC-approved investors purchase fractional ownership of tokenized real-estate projects (Real-World Assets). Ownership is represented as ERC-4626 vault shares on the Base blockchain.

Bridge is used as a licensed third-party money-movement / on-ramp provider so that investors can fund purchases with USD (ACH, wire, or card). Bridge converts those funds to USDC on Base and delivers them to Sanova’s designated treasury wallet. Sanova then allocates USDC into the corresponding property vault and mints vault shares to the investor.

Investor funds received via Bridge are used only to:

  • Settle the investor’s purchase of RWA vault shares
  • Allocate USDC into the property SPV vault that backs the asset
  • Pay platform / advisor commissions on-chain in USDC

Funds are not used for FX speculation, crypto trading, peer-to-peer transfers, or unrelated third-party payouts.

2. Direct answers for Bridge review

Will received funds be exchanged for another currency?
Yes. Investor USD (or local fiat collected through Bridge) is converted by Bridge into USDC. Sanova does not operate an FX desk; Bridge performs the conversion. Final settlement asset accepted by Sanova is always USDC on Base.
Where will funds ultimately be sent?
1) Primary destination: Sanova USDC Treasury wallet on Base (0xa993743CFB85E8d6481Ef60bb3D397F49604A592)
2) Then allocated on-chain to the property ERC-4626 vault (SPV compartment)
3) Investor receives vault shares in their linked Base wallet
4) A small fee portion (~3–5%) goes to platform / advisor commission wallets on Base

7. Example transaction

  1. Investor buys USD 1,000 of vault shares (+ fees ≈ USD 1,050).
  2. Investor pays USD 1,050 via Bridge (ACH, wire, or card).
  3. Bridge converts to ~1,050 USDC and sends them to the Sanova treasury on Base.
  4. Sanova verifies the on-chain receipt, then allocates ~1,000 USDC to the property vault and ~50 USDC to commissions.
  5. Investor receives ERC-4626 vault shares in their linked wallet.

8. Compliance controls

  • Investor KYC required (Didit) before purchase / Bridge funding
  • Single settlement asset: USDC on Base
  • Treasury-first: shares minted only after on-chain USDC verification
  • Bridge webhooks reconciled against treasury transaction hashes
  • Payment intents, Bridge customer / transfer IDs, and tx hashes retained for audit
